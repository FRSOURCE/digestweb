## Summary & Key Takeaways

Chad Whitacre is retiring from the tech industry and open source, citing AI as the primary reason.
He describes his decision as becoming "AI Amish," aiming for a 1980s-level of technological engagement.
His previous experience with Claude Code Opus 4.5 left him feeling "intoxicated" and later "weirded out."
He felt like he had another "person" in his head, a computer system owned by a megacorp.
The decision is a commitment to disembarking from "technological accelerationism."
He compares his choice to the Amish preserving a way of life that might be needed again.

## Our Commentary

This hits hard. I've seen the "I'm quitting tech because of AI" sentiment floating around, but this is someone actually doing it, and with such a clear, almost manifesto-like statement. The "AI Amish" concept is striking. I can absolutely relate to the feeling of something being "off" after deep immersion with an agentic AI; it's a strange, almost invasive intimacy. We're all grappling with the pace of change, but this is a stark reminder that for some, the cost to their well-being is too high. It makes me wonder about the long-term psychological toll of constant AI interaction.
