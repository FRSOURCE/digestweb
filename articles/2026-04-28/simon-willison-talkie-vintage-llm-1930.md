## Summary & Key Takeaways

- Simon Willison has unveiled 'talkie,' a whimsical 13-billion parameter "vintage language model."
- The model is presented as if it originated from 1930, offering a satirical look at AI.
- 'Talkie' generates text in a style reminiscent of early 20th-century prose, using archaic vocabulary and grammar.
- The project uses this playful premise to explore the rapid evolution of AI technology.
- It prompts reflection on the nature of language and our current perceptions of machine intelligence.
- The article serves as a clever commentary on the hype and historical context surrounding AI development.

## Our Commentary

This is exactly the kind of creative, thought-provoking content we love to see in the AI space. Simon Willison consistently delivers unique perspectives, and 'talkie' is a brilliant piece of satire. It forces us to step back and consider how our understanding of "intelligence" and "language models" is shaped by our current technological context. Imagining an LLM from 1930 highlights just how far we've come, but also how much our foundational questions about AI remain the same. It's a refreshing break from the usual "bigger model, better performance" narrative.
