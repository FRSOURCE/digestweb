## Summary & Key Takeaways

- Anthropic has been facing criticism from developers regarding its model performance.
- Developers report experiencing a "dumber" version of Anthropic's models.
- Access to Claude Code has been removed for some paid accounts, causing further frustration.
- The article speculates that recent capacity shortages, despite securing compute from SpaceX, might be the underlying reason for these issues.
- This situation highlights potential challenges in scaling AI services and maintaining developer trust.

## Our Commentary

This article touches on a deeply unsettling trend we've observed in the AI space: the perceived degradation of model quality and the arbitrary removal of features, especially for paying customers. If Anthropic's actions are indeed driven by capacity shortages, it underscores the immense infrastructure challenges facing even well-funded AI companies. It's a stark reminder that the "AI gold rush" isn't without its growing pains, and developers are often the first to feel the impact. We genuinely hope for more transparency from providers when such issues arise, as trust is easily eroded when core tools become unreliable or inaccessible.
