## Summary & Key Takeaways

- The atproto protocol fundamentally differs from traditional social networks.
- It operates without the concept of "instances" in the way Mastodon or other federated systems do.
- Data portability and user control are central to its design philosophy.
- This architectural choice has significant implications for decentralization and censorship resistance.

## Our Commentary

Dan Abramov always makes me think. The idea of "no instances" in a decentralized protocol is a fascinating distinction. It makes me wonder about the practical implications for scaling and moderation. This is a deep cut, but a good one.
