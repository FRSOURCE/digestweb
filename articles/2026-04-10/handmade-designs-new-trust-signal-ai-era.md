## Summary & Key Takeaways

- NNGroup identifies "handmade designs" as an emerging trust signal for users.
- In an era saturated with AI-generated content, users are experiencing "AI fatigue."
- There's a growing preference for designs that visibly appear to be crafted by a human.
- This shift impacts design strategy, emphasizing authenticity and human connection.

This is a really insightful observation from NNGroup, and it resonates deeply with me. We've been talking about the "uncanny valley" in AI for a while, but this points to a broader "AI fatigue" in design. As AI becomes ubiquitous, the human touch, the imperfections, the _soul_ in design, might become the ultimate differentiator and a powerful signal of authenticity and trust. It's a fascinating counter-trend to the push for AI-driven efficiency and could lead to a resurgence of artisanal digital design.
