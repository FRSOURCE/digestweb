## Summary & Key Takeaways

- NNGroup highlights the emerging reality of AI agents interacting with digital interfaces alongside humans.
- This development necessitates a fundamental re-evaluation of what constitutes a "user" in design.
- Designing for both human and AI users requires a renewed focus on accessibility principles.
- The article explores the implications for future UX design and interface development.

This is a truly paradigm-shifting concept, and NNGroup is spot on for highlighting it. The idea of "AI agents as users" fundamentally changes how we approach interface design and accessibility. It's not just about humans anymore; we need to consider how an autonomous agent will parse, understand, and interact with our digital products. This will push us to create more structured, semantic, and robust interfaces, which, ironically, will likely benefit human users too. This is a critical area of research and development for the future of the web.
