## Summary & Key Takeaways

- The article explores `Standard.site`, a userland schema for content standardization.
- It is designed for the `atproto` protocol, notably used by the Bluesky social network.
- The schema aims to define consistent structures for content types like 'publication' and 'document.'
- This initiative supports interoperability within decentralized social platforms.

## Our Commentary

The decentralized web is still finding its footing, and efforts like `Standard.site` are crucial for building any kind of interoperability. It's a complex space, and I appreciate Mat's cautious optimism. We need more agreed-upon standards if these platforms are ever going to truly flourish.
