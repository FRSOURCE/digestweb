## Summary & Key Takeaways

- The article advocates for viewing accessibility as an operational capability.
- It challenges the traditional approach of treating it as a mere feature or compliance item.
- Teams must guarantee usability, security, and maintainability alongside speed.
- Integrating accessibility throughout the development process is crucial.
- The piece explores practical implications of this shift in mindset.

## Our Commentary

This is a message we need to hear constantly. Treating accessibility as an afterthought or a checkbox item is a disservice to users and often leads to more work down the line. Framing it as an "operational capability" really hits home. It's about baked-in quality, not just a bolt-on. I genuinely believe this mindset shift is essential for building truly inclusive web experiences.
