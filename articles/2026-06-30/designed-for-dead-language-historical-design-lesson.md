## Summary & Key Takeaways

- Modern language apps often inherit teaching methods originally designed for Latin.
- This historical context provides a valuable design lesson.
- The Prussian Method, developed for grading language at scale, became dominant.
- It focused on grammar rules and written comprehension, not oral skills.
- The original constraints that shaped the method became invisible over time.

## Our Commentary

This is a fascinating historical deep-dive. It's a powerful reminder that design decisions, even seemingly modern ones, often carry the baggage of past constraints. It makes you wonder what "dead language" paradigms we're still unconsciously applying in web development or AI. A good prompt for critical thinking.
