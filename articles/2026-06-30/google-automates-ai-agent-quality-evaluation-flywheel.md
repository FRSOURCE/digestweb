## Summary & Key Takeaways

- Google addresses the challenge of maintaining AI agent quality.
- A new developer skill automates a five-stage evaluation flywheel for coding agents.
- The process includes data preparation, inference, and grading with AutoRaters.
- It also involves analyzing failure clusters and targeted optimizations.
- The tool runs continuously against production or on-demand with synthetic scenarios.
- Developers can describe testing goals in plain language for validation.

## Our Commentary

This is exactly the kind of tooling we need for AI agents. The "prompt tweak roulette" problem is real, and having an automated evaluation flywheel is a huge step towards more robust agent development. I'm particularly interested in the "adaptive AutoRaters" – that sounds like a powerful concept. This feels like a foundational piece for building truly reliable AI systems.
