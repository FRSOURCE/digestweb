## Summary & Key Takeaways

- Paul Graham observes a rise in AI-written emails from founders.
- He identifies AI-generated content by its distinct "journalistic style."
- Graham states he never finishes reading AI-written emails from humans.
- He views AI-assisted writing as a form of deception.
- Suggests it reflects poorly on the author's ability and integrity.

## Our Commentary

Paul Graham's take here resonates deeply. We've all seen the bland, perfectly structured, yet utterly soulless AI prose. It's not just about efficiency; it's about authenticity. When you realize you're reading something generated, it instantly creates a barrier. I think this sentiment will only grow as AI writing becomes more ubiquitous and less distinguishable.
