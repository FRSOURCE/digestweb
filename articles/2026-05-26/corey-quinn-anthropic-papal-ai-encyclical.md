## Summary & Key Takeaways

- Corey Quinn comments on Anthropic's influence on the Pope's AI encyclical.

- Specifically references Christopher Olah's role in "Magnifica Humanitas."
- Highlights the intersection of AI ethics, corporate influence, and religious authority.

## Our Commentary

Corey Quinn's quote here is just _chef's kiss_. "Getting the literal Pope to canonize your product's specific technical limitations as a spiritual treatise" is a line that will stick with me. It's a darkly humorous, yet deeply unsettling, commentary on the lengths companies will go to shape the narrative around AI ethics. The implications of this kind of influence are pretty wild.
