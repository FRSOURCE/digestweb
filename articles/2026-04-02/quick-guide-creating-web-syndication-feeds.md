## Summary & Key Takeaways

- The article provides a guide to creating and understanding web syndication feeds, such as RSS and Atom.
- It argues that RSS is still highly relevant and essential for proper content publishing on the web.
- The guide focuses on the core principles of various feed formats rather than exhaustive details.
- It serves as a reference or cheat sheet for developers, even if most tools automate feed generation.
- Understanding feed formats is crucial for troubleshooting and parsing content, not just creating it.
- The article includes boilerplate and metadata requirements for RSS feeds as an example.

## Our Commentary

We're big fans of this article's premise: RSS is not dead! It's a fundamental part of the open web, and it's refreshing to see a guide that champions its continued relevance.
In an era dominated by algorithmic feeds, the ability to subscribe directly to content creators via RSS is a powerful act of reclaiming control over one's information diet.
This guide is excellent for anyone looking to ensure their content is truly "on the web" and accessible beyond walled gardens. It's a foundational piece of knowledge that every web publisher should have.
