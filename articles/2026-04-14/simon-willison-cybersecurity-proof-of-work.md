## Summary & Key Takeaways

- Simon Willison presents an analysis suggesting that cybersecurity is evolving to resemble a "Proof of Work" system.
- This perspective implies that digital defense increasingly requires demonstrable effort and computational expense to secure systems.
- The commentary likely explores the implications of this shift for both attackers and defenders in the digital realm.
- It offers a unique lens through which to understand the escalating challenges in maintaining online security.

## Our Commentary

Simon Willison always has a knack for cutting through the noise and offering insightful analogies. The idea that cybersecurity is becoming a "Proof of Work" problem is a compelling one. It suggests an arms race where the cost of defense is constantly rising, mirroring the computational effort required in blockchain. This perspective is particularly relevant as AI enters the fray, potentially escalating the "work" required on both sides. It's a sobering thought, but one that feels increasingly accurate.
