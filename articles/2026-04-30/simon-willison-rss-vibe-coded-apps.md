## Summary & Key Takeaways

- Simon Willison proposes that RSS is essential for sharing "vibe-coded apps."
- The article suggests a need for decentralized content distribution mechanisms for a new type of application.
- It advocates for the enduring relevance of RSS in a world of abundant, perhaps more ephemeral or niche, digital creations.

## Our Commentary

"Vibe-coded apps" is a fascinating concept, and Simon Willison's call for RSS to power their distribution is a classic Willison take. We've seen the web become increasingly centralized, and the idea of using RSS to share more personal, perhaps less commercial, applications resonates with the open web ethos. It's a reminder that sometimes the old tools are the best tools, especially when trying to foster a more diverse and less platform-dependent ecosystem. I'm curious to see if this idea gains traction.
