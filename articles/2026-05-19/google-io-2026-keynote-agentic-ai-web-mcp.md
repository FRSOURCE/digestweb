## Summary & Key Takeaways

- Google I/O 2026 signals a transition from assistive AI to independent agents.
- The Gemini 3.5 series and Antigravity agent-first platform were launched.
- New Android CLI tools and an automated Migration agent were introduced.
- Web development sees Chrome DevTools for agents and HTML-in-Canvas API.
- WebMCP, an open web standard for browser-based AI agents, is proposed.

## Our Commentary

This is the big one from Google I/O. The shift to "independent agents" feels like a massive leap, and the Antigravity platform sounds like Google's answer to orchestrating this new paradigm. WebMCP as an open standard for browser agents is particularly intriguing – it suggests a future where agents are deeply integrated into the web itself. I'm trying to wrap my head around the implications of all this.
