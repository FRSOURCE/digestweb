## Summary & Key Takeaways

- The article explores writing as a process of sustained attention and endurance, not just sentence production.
- It argues that LLMs can reproduce the _appearance_ of writing but not the transformative learning that occurs during its making.
- The author emphasizes the importance of moving through language without an AI intermediary.
- Revision is presented as an act of generously imagining and "revising up" the reader, which in turn elevates the writer.

## Our Commentary

This piece really resonates with me. In an age where AI can churn out text at an alarming rate, it's crucial to remember what makes human creation valuable. The idea that writing is "the training of endurance by way of sustained attention" and that revision is about "revising your reader up" is a beautiful and profound counter-narrative to the instant gratification promised by AI tools. It's a reminder that the _process_ is often as important as the product, and that's something AI can't yet replicate.
