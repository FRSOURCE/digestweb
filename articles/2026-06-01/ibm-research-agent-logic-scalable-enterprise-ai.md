## Summary & Key Takeaways

- IBM Research emphasizes the importance of agent logic for enterprise AI.
- Scalable AI adoption requires capabilities beyond standalone LLMs.
- Agent logic enables more robust and autonomous AI systems.
- The article discusses the architectural shift needed for enterprise-grade AI.
- It highlights the need for AI to perform complex, multi-step tasks reliably.

## Our Commentary

This is the direction I've been expecting AI to go. LLMs are powerful, but they're just one piece of the puzzle. The idea of 'agent logic' for scalable enterprise adoption feels right; it's about orchestrating these models to actually _do_ things reliably. There's something unsettling about agents churning away at 3am while nobody's watching, but the potential for automation is undeniable. We need to get the guardrails right.
