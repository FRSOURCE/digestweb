## Summary & Key Takeaways

- Microsoft Research has developed Vega for zero-knowledge proofs.
- Vega converts full digital credentials into concise proofs.
- It ensures only essential information is shared, enhancing privacy.
- The system is designed for practical performance in real-world applications.
- This technology is particularly relevant for digital identity in the age of AI.

## Our Commentary

Zero-knowledge proofs are one of those technologies that feel like they're always "just around the corner" for mainstream adoption. Vega sounds like a practical step towards making them usable for digital identity. In an AI-driven world, privacy and selective disclosure are more important than ever. I'm hopeful this pushes ZKPs further into the mainstream.
