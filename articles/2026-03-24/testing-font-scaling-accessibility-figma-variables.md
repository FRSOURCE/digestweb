## Summary & Key Takeaways

- The article demonstrates how to test font scaling for accessibility using Figma variables.
- It advocates for integrating accessibility testing seamlessly into everyday design workflows.
- The approach aims to make accessibility an inherent part of the design process rather than an afterthought.

## Our Commentary

This is exactly the kind of practical accessibility advice we love to see. Integrating font scaling tests directly into Figma with variables is a brilliant way to make accessibility "feel almost inevitable rather than optional." Far too often, accessibility is treated as a separate, late-stage concern, leading to costly reworks. By embedding these checks into the design phase, teams can catch issues much earlier and build more inclusive experiences from the ground up. It's a great example of how thoughtful tooling and workflow design can significantly improve the quality of our digital products.
