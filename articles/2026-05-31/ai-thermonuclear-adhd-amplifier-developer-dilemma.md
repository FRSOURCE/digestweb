## Summary & Key Takeaways

- The author questions the true value of constant AI tool usage.
- AI can quickly generate numerous projects, but maintaining them becomes an issue.
- The technology is described as a "thermonuclear ADHD amplifier" due to its impact on focus.
- Many AI-generated "quick scripts" often fail to solve the original problem.
- The critical skill for managing AI might be discipline, which is hard to cultivate.
- Some users with ADHD, however, report AI helps them achieve focus and complete projects.

## Our Commentary

This hits hard. I've felt this exact pull, the endless rabbit hole of 'just one more prompt.' Simon Willison articulating the 'thermonuclear ADHD amplifier' effect is chillingly accurate. We're all building so much, so fast, but what are we actually _finishing_? And what are we truly _caring_ for? The idea that discipline is the solution... well, I'm right there with him, still trying to figure that out after decades. It's a messy problem, and I don't think there's an easy answer.
