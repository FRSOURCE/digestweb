## Summary & Key Takeaways

- Deno v2.8.2 adds post-quantum cryptographic algorithms like ML-DSA and ML-KEM.
- The `deno compile` command now features improved bundling and minification.
- Node.js compatibility is enhanced with better `node:http` and `node:https` support.
- The Jupyter kernel has been rewritten in JavaScript, removing external dependencies.
- LSP now offers autocomplete for `jsr:`, `npm:`, and `node:` imports.
- New `--env-file` flag is available for Deno tasks.

## Our Commentary

Deno keeps pushing. Post-quantum crypto in a runtime is a big deal for future-proofing, even if it feels a bit ahead of its time for most web devs. The `deno compile` improvements are also genuinely useful for deployment. I'm always impressed by how quickly they iterate and integrate cutting-edge features. It makes me wonder if Node.js will ever catch up on some of these fronts.
