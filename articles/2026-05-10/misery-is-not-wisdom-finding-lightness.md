## Summary & Key Takeaways

- The author reflects on the challenge of maintaining optimism and "lightness" in a world often filled with negativity.
- Drawing inspiration from Italo Calvino, the post argues that lightness is a crucial quality not just for writers but for life in general.
- It's about facing difficult realities with hope, not ignoring them.
- The author shares specific examples that have brought them lightness, including the new XOXO website and GrilliType's GT Mechanik website, praising their innovative design.
- The post also briefly mentions the AppleTV show "Widow's Bay" as another source of lightness.

## Our Commentary

This is a refreshing read. In an industry often obsessed with the next big thing or the latest performance metric, it's easy to get bogged down. Robin's call for "lightness" resonates deeply. It's not about being naive, but about finding joy and inspiration even amidst the noise. The specific web design examples, like GrilliType's interactive scroll, are a great reminder that creativity and pushing boundaries are still alive and well on the web. We appreciate the human touch and the reminder to look beyond the purely technical.
